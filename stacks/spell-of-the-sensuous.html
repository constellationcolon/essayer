On Language

Greeks: humans are apart from animals, men are apart from women & children => justification for special place in society & the world. (i.e. justification for exploitation and domination)

Darwin: humans aren’t that special, we’re descended from apes. => Human language as replacement justification, emphasis on abstract formalisms of language rather than the metaphorical nature of utterances.

Abram, D. (2012). The Spell of the Sensuous: Perception and Language in a More-Than-Human World. Knopf Doubleday Publishing Group.
